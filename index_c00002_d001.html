    Beehive Monitoring System Overview

    The system consists of a central control unit, a sensor module, and a power module housed in a waterproof IP66 enclosure with an external WiFi antenna. It supports WiFi connectivity and OTA firmware updates. Measurements include battery voltage, temperatures (inside and outside), humidity, air pressure, light intensity, and sound samples, with derived data such as height estimation from pressure, sound intensity, and frequency spectrum analysis using STFT. Powered by a 4400mAh lithium battery with solar panel recharging, it uses a BME280 and MAX9814 sensor for environmental and audio data. The "Spaarkast" variant includes an isolation board and is painted with stand oil paint for durability.

    Temperature

    In a beehive, temperature regulation is crucial for the colony’s survival, especially in winter. Bees do not heat the entire hive but focus on maintaining warmth within their cluster, where temperatures can reach around 35°C at the center. As heat rises, the area immediately above the cluster remains warmer than other parts of the hive, making overhead insulation beneficial for reducing heat loss. Contrary to common belief, leaving a super of honey on the hive does not burden the bees with extra heating; instead, it acts as an insulator and stabilizes internal temperatures. Additionally, the honey near the cluster remains warm enough for consumption, ensuring the bees have accessible food throughout colder months.
